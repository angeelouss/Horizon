hackernews · aurenvale · Jun 27, 09:18 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48696585)

**Background**: Large language models (LLMs) are AI models that generate human-like text, typically in an autoregressive manner, meaning they predict one token at a time. Speculative decoding is an optimization technique for these models where a smaller, faster "draft" model proposes several candidate tokens, which are then quickly verified by the larger, more accurate "target" model in a single pass, significantly speeding up the generation process without altering the output quality.
